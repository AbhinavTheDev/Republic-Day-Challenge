Indian Constitution: Articles and Amendments

Major Articles

Article Description
Article 1 Name and territory of the Union
Article 12 Definition of the State; Expands the scope of 'State' for enforcement of Fundamental Rights
Article 15 Prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth
Article 21 Protection of life and personal liberty; Guarantees the right to life and personal liberty
Article 32 Remedies for enforcement of fundamental rights; Provides the right to move the Supreme Court for the enforcement of rights
Article 44 Uniform civil code for the citizens; Aims at a common set of laws governing personal matters for all citizens
Article 370 Special status to Jammu and Kashmir; Provides autonomy to the state of Jammu and Kashmir (now abrogated)
Article 356 President's Rule in states; Allows the President to assume control of the state government in certain circumstances

Major Amendments

Amendment Description
1st Amendment (1951) Addition of Ninth Schedule; Protection to land reform laws from judicial review
42nd Amendment (1976) Constitutional emergency powers; Expanded the powers of the executive during a state of emergency
44th Amendment (1978) Restrictions on the right to property; Removed the right to property as a Fundamental Right
73rd Amendment (1992) Panchayati Raj Institutions; Provides constitutional status to Panchayati Raj Institutions
74th Amendment (1992) Municipalities; Provides constitutional status to Municipalities
86th Amendment (2002) Right to Education as a Fundamental Right; Added Article 21A to provide free and compulsory education for children
